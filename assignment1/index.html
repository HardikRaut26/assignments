Few facts about the Indian Army

The Indian Army is the land-based branch of the Indian Armed Forces, responsible for safeguarding the nation's territorial integrity and sovereignty. It was officially established on January 15, 1949, and is one of the largest standing armies in the world. The Army plays a key role in disaster relief, counter-insurgency, and peacekeeping operations. Known for its discipline, valor, and sacrifice, it upholds the motto "Service Before Self."
